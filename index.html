Dialogue Edit

This often un-mentioned yet vital part of the post-production process is the first step in a sound edit. I love taking the sound recorded on set, and through microphone choices, editing, sound shaping and adding ambience and room tone, creating something clean, watchable and ready for sound design and music to be added.

Sound Design

The creative elements of a soundtrack can add so much to the ambience of a project. Correct sound design is vital to tell the story. If you get it right it will be as important as picture or dialogue. I am very familiar with adding sound effects or creating Foley sounds if and when needed.

Re-Recording Mixer

Often also referred to as a Dubbing Mixer, this is where all the sound elements are balanced, panned and effected to create the overall final audio mix of the film. I have done several stereo mixes on features that have been ready for premiers and film festivals, however a surround sound mix will need to be achieved elsewhere.
